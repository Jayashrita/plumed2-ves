\page ves_theory Theory of VES

In Variationally Enhanced Sampling \cite Valsson-PRL-2014 an external biasing potential \f$V(\mathbf{s})\f$ in some space of collective variables (CVs) \f$s\f$ is constructed by minimizing the following convex functional
\f[
\Omega[V] = \frac{1}{\beta} \log
\frac{\int d\mathbf{s} \, e^{-\beta [F(\mathbf{s}+V(\mathbf{s}))]}}
{\int d\mathbf{s} \, e^{-\beta F(\mathbf{s}}}
+ \int d\mathbf{s} \, p(\mathbf{s}) V(\mathbf{s}),
\f]
where \f$F(\mathbf{s})\f$ is the free energy surface (FES) associated to the CVs at temperature \f$T\f$ and \f$p(\mathbf{s})\f$ is a so-called target distribution that is assumed to be normalized. It can be shown that the minimum of \f$\Omega[V]\f$ is given by
\f[
V(\mathbf{s}) = - F(\mathbf{s}) - \frac{1}{\beta} \log p(\mathbf{s}).
\f]
